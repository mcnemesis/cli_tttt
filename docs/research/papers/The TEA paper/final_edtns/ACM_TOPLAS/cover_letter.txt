Dear Editors of ACM Transactions on Programming Languages and Systems (TOPLAS),

I am pleased to submit our manuscript entitled “TEA: Transforming Executable Alphabet – A Minimal Language Leveraging Sequence Transformer Chaining” for consideration in TOPLAS. This paper introduces TEA, a compact general‑purpose programming language designed around a sequence‑transformer‑chaining paradigm, optimized for problem solving via text processing. We present the language’s design, formal semantics, implementation strategies, and representative applications, alongside an evaluation of its expressiveness, concision, and cross‑platform performance.

We believe this work aligns closely with the scope of TOPLAS, as it contributes to programming language design, semantics, and implementation. TEA’s minimal instruction set, based on the Latin alphabet, offers a novel approach to language concision and memorability, while its cross‑platform interpreters and embedded primitives demonstrate practical applicability. We also highlight TEA’s potential roles in education, low‑resource mobile programming, and domain‑specific sequence analysis, making it relevant both to theoretical and applied programming language research.

This manuscript is original, has not been published elsewhere, and is not under consideration by any other journal. All authors have approved the submission. We have carefully followed ACM’s formatting requirements, including CCS Concepts and keywords, to ensure compliance with submission standards.

We appreciate your consideration of our work and look forward to the opportunity to contribute to the ongoing dialogue in programming language research through TOPLAS.
